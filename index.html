About me

I am a 4th year graduate student in Planetary Science, California Institute of Technology. As a planetary physicist, I'm interested in geophysical fluid dynamics, moist convection and atmospheric photochemistry. I develop high performance software to aid my research. I have written a parallel quasi-geostrophic dynamical core, a shallow water core and a primitive equation solver in an axisymmetric geometry using C++. I also enjoy designing inversion algorithms to retrieve atmospheric properties from remote sensing. I'm currently developing retrieval algorithms for the Juno mission, which will arrive at Jupiter in July, 2016. My CV is available for download at [here].

Research

My primary research interest is geophysical fluid dynamics. Classic fluid dynamics focuses on linear instabilities, turbulent energy cascade and wave-mean flow interactions. My taste of geophysical fluid dynamics lies in the forcing terms, which are moist convection and radiation/photochemistry. I like to develop a hierachy of numerical models with increasing complexity to unravel the mystery folded inside the nonlinear Navier-Stokes equations.

Code

In my spare time, I write numerical codes for both research and educational purposes.I build a numerial toolkit named "Armada" to solve problems in atmospheric science. Currently Armada comprises four parts:

  • A radiative module that calculates radiances and heating/cooling ratio using both line-by-line method and automatic correlated-k method. The radiative transfer program in the backend is c-disort.
  • A dynamic module that solves partial differential equations using finite volumn method. I uses approximate Riemann solvers (e.g. Roe linearization) and flux limiter method to obtain fast speed and high accuracy. Finite volumn formulation ensures the model to be stable as long as the CFL criterion is satisfied and no explicit diffsion or hyperviscosity is required.
  • A thermodynamic module that calculates phase transitions of condensible species. It uses consistent entropy formulation and can resolve triple-point equilibrium. I don't use any approximation during the calculation so that program works from a dry atmosphere to a steam atmosphere.
  • A photochemical module that is still underconstruction.
